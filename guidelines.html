At LIT, we prioritize keeping your education affordable and efficient, so you can accelerate your path to graduation, expedite degree completion and start your career. This summer, LIT is offering the opportunity to add one additional course during the Summer 2026 semester at no cost!

Already registered for Summer 2026?

Students who are already enrolled in Summer 2026 courses may qualify to add an additional class to their existing schedule for FREE. The free course must be added to an existing summer schedule and eligibility applies. To be considered, complete the Summer Free Course form as soon as possible.

Not registered yet for Summer 2026?

New and returning students who register for two or more Summer 2026 classes may qualify to receive a FREE class. Students must enroll in more than one summer course to be eligible. Once registered, complete the Summer Free Course form to apply for consideration.

Funds are limited and free courses will be awarded on a first-come, first-served basis. Eligibility is determined using need-based criteria to help reduce students' out-of-pocket expenses.

Don't miss this opportunity to save money, stay on track, and complete your degree faster. Register for summer classes today!

More ways to maximize your money in the Summer and Fall 2026 semester:

  • Complete 2025-2026 FAFSA at fafsa.gov.
  • Complete your LIT scholarship application at lit.edu/foundation/scholarships.
  • Meet with a Financial Aid Specialist to review your financial options.
  • Dual credit students qualify for courses at a discounted rate during the summer immediately following graduation, or while enrolled in high school.

Basic guidelines for web.

Recommendations for Web Images

  • File Size: Ideally under 100KB-500KB for best performance
  • Large Dimensions: 1000px-1400px (width)
  • Medium Dimensions: 600x400px
  • Format: JPEG for photos, PNG for graphics needing transparency, and WebP for better compression
  • Resolution: 72 DPI (dots per inch) is standard for web, as higher (300 DPI) only increases size without improving screen quality.
  • Aspect Ratio: A 3:2 or 16:9 ratio is best for standard viewing across devices.
