Purpose Statement

The Office of Institutional Effectiveness and Assessment at Lamar Institute of Technology (LIT) empowers institutional decision-making by providing accurate, timely, and relevant data-along with meaningful analysis and interpretation. Through a systematic approach to assessment and planning, the office supports continuous improvement in student learning and institutional operations. This integrated process enhances the effectiveness of LIT's programs, services, and strategic initiatives, ensuring optimal outcomes for students and the broader campus community.

Robin Clements

Assessment and Accreditation Manager

Robin Clements serves as Accreditation and Assessment Manager (2023-present), supporting institutional effectiveness, assessment, and accreditation initiatives. She collaborates with academic and administrative units to align assessment practices with strategic priorities, provides guidance on outcomes development and assessment methodologies in accordance with SACSCOC standards, and supports continuous improvement through data-informed analysis and reporting. Her work includes facilitating program reviews, supporting general education assessment focused on student success, and contributing to institutional planning through presentations and reports for internal and external stakeholders.

Clements brings 17 years of experience in higher education, including prior roles in institutional research and effectiveness, academic affairs, financial aid, and student support services at Lamar State College Orange and Lamar State College Port Arthur. She holds a Bachelor of Science in Business Management from Colorado State University and an Associate of Applied Science in Management Development Business Administration from Lamar State College-Port Arthur and is a member of the Kappa Beta Delta and Phi Theta Kappa honor societies.

Miguel Garcia

Institutional Research Analyst

Miguel Rodriguez has served as a Reporting Analyst in the Institutional Effectiveness and Assessment Department since January 2024. Originally from Spain, he brings a decade of experience as a Sports Analyst with the Spanish Olympic Committee. He relocated to Beaumont, Texas, five years ago and has since held roles as an Administrative Specialist at St. Elizabeth Hospital and as an elementary school teacher.

Dr Bishar Sethna

Exec. Dir. of Institutional Effectiveness & Assessment

Dr. Bishar Sethna has served as Executive Director at Lamar Institute of Technology since 2021, where he leads initiatives focused on academic quality, operational effectiveness, accreditation compliance, and data-informed decision-making through interactive Tableau dashboards. He is committed to advancing educational excellence through evidence-based practices and continuous improvement.

Dr. Sethna began his career in institutional research in 1994 as Director of Institutional Research at Lamar State College-Orange, a role he held for over 20 years. He later served as Senior Research and Accreditation Analyst at Lamar University before assuming his current leadership position. He is an active member of the Texas Association of Institutional Research and has served on the TAIR Conference Planning Committee.

In addition to his administrative leadership, Dr. Sethna has taught mathematics and business courses, including algebra and salesmanship, with teaching interests in personal finance and investments. He holds a Doctor of Education in Educational Leadership from Lamar University, an MBA in Finance from Fort Hays State University, and a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from the University of Bombay.
